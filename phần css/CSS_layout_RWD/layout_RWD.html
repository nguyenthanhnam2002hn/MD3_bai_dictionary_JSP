    Brazil’s supreme court has delivered a historic win for Indigenous land rights by rejecting a move backed by agribusiness to limit those rights. The court’s decision, with nine out of 11 judges voting against it, prevented the enforcement of a ‘time limit trick’ aimed at preventing Indigenous communities from claiming land they hadn’t physically occupied in 1988. The ruling was celebrated across Brazil, especially in the Amazon region, home to half of the country’s 1.7 million Indigenous citizens. Indigenous leaders and activists expressed their joy and relief, emphasizing the importance of this victory for their history and rights.
